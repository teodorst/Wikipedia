==Events==
* [[624]] &ndash; [[Battle of Badr]]: a key battle between [[Muhammad]]'s army &ndash; the new followers of Islam and the [[Quraysh]] of [[Mecca]]. The Muslims won this battle, known as the turning point of Islam, which took place in the [[Hejaz]] region of western [[Arabia]].
* [[874]] &ndash; The bones of [[Saint Nicephorus]] are interred in the [[Church of the Holy Apostles]], [[Constantinople]].
*[[1138]] &ndash; Cardinal Gregorio Conti is elected [[Antipope]] as [[Antipope Victor IV (1138)|Victor IV]], succeeding [[Antipope Anacletus II|Anacletus II]].
*[[1591]] &ndash; [[Battle of Tondibi]]: In [[Mali]], [[Morocco|Moroccan]] forces of the [[Saadi dynasty]] led by Judar Pasha defeat the [[Songhai Empire]], despite being outnumbered by at least five to one.
*[[1639]] &ndash; [[Harvard College]] is named after clergyman [[John Harvard (clergyman)|John Harvard]].
*[[1697]] &ndash; [[Nojpetén]], capital of the last independent [[Itza people|Maya]] kingdom, fell to Spanish [[conquistador]]s, the final step in the [[Spanish conquest of Guatemala]].
*[[1781]] &ndash; [[William Herschel]] discovers [[Uranus]].
*[[1809]] &ndash; [[Gustav IV Adolf of Sweden]] is deposed in a [[coup d'état]].
*[[1845]] &ndash; [[Felix Mendelssohn]]'s [[Violin Concerto (Mendelssohn)|Violin Concerto]] receives its première performance in [[Leipzig]] with [[Ferdinand David (musician)|Ferdinand David]] as soloist.
*[[1862]] &ndash; [[American Civil War]]: The U.S. [[Federal government of the United States|federal government]] forbids all [[Union (American Civil War)|Union]] army officers from returning fugitive slaves, thus effectively annulling the [[Fugitive Slave Act of 1850]] and setting the stage for the [[Emancipation Proclamation]].
*[[1865]] &ndash; American Civil War: The [[Confederate States of America]] agree to the use of [[African-American]] troops.
*[[1881]] &ndash; [[Alexander II of Russia]] is killed near his palace when a bomb is thrown at him. (Gregorian date: it was March 1 in the [[Julian calendar]] then in use in Russia.)
*[[1884]] &ndash; The [[Siege of Khartoum]], [[Sudan]] begins, ending on January 26, 1885.
*[[1897]] &ndash; [[San Diego State University]] is founded.
*[[1900]] &ndash; [[Second Boer War]]: British forces occupy [[Bloemfontein]], [[Orange Free State]].
*[[1920]] &ndash; The [[Kapp Putsch]] briefly ousts the [[Weimar Republic]] government from Berlin.
*[[1921]] &ndash; [[Mongolia]] is proclaimed an independent monarchy, ruled by Russian military officer [[Roman von Ungern-Sternberg]] as a dictator.
*[[1930]] &ndash; The news of the discovery of [[Pluto]] is telegraphed to the [[Harvard College Observatory]].
*[[1933]] &ndash; [[Great Depression]]: Banks in the U.S. begin to re-open after [[President of the United States|President]] [[Franklin D. Roosevelt]] mandates a "[[bank holiday]]".
*[[1938]] &ndash; ''[[World News Roundup]]'' is broadcast for the first time on [[CBS Radio]] in the United States.
*[[1940]] &ndash; The Russo-Finnish [[Winter War]] ends.
*[[1943]] &ndash; [[The Holocaust]]: [[Nazi Germany|German]] forces liquidate the [[Kraków Ghetto|Jewish ghetto in Kraków]].
*[[1954]] &ndash; [[First Indochina War]]: [[Viet Minh]] forces under [[Võ Nguyên Giáp]] unleashed a massive artillery barrage on the French to begin the [[Battle of Dien Bien Phu|Battle of Điện Biên Phủ]], the climactic battle in the First Indochina War.
*[[1957]] &ndash; [[Cuba]]n student revolutionaries storm the [[Museum of the Revolution (Cuba)|presidential palace]] in [[Havana]] in a failed attempt on the life of [[List of Presidents of Cuba|President]] [[Fulgencio Batista]].
*[[1962]] &ndash; [[Lyman Lemnitzer]], chairman of the [[Joint Chiefs of Staff]], delivers a proposal, called [[Operation Northwoods]], regarding performing [[terrorist attacks]] upon [[Guantanamo Bay Naval Base]], to [[United States Secretary of Defense|Secretary of Defense]] [[Robert McNamara]]. The proposal is scrapped and President [[John F. Kennedy]] removes Lemnitzer from his position.
*[[1963]] &ndash; Police in [[Phoenix, Arizona]] arrest [[Ernesto Miranda]] and charge him with kidnap and rape.  His conviction is ultimately set aside by the [[United States Supreme Court]] in ''[[Miranda v. Arizona]]''
*[[1964]] &ndash; American [[Murder of Kitty Genovese|Kitty Genovese]] is murdered, the media erroneously report that many of the victim's neighbours witnessed the crime yet failed to help, prompting research into the [[bystander effect]].
*[[1969]] &ndash; [[Apollo program]]: [[Apollo 9]] returns safely to [[Earth]] after testing the [[Apollo Lunar Module|Lunar Module]].
*[[1979]] &ndash; The [[New Jewel Movement]], headed by [[Maurice Bishop]], ousts [[Prime Minister of Grenada|Prime Minister]] [[Eric Gairy]] in a nearly bloodless [[coup d'état]] in [[Grenada]].
*[[1985]] &ndash; [[1985 Kenilworth Road riot|The Kenilworth Road riot]] takes place at an association football match at [[Kenilworth Road]] in [[Luton]], England with disturbances before, during and after an [[FA Cup]] 6th Round tie between [[Luton Town F.C.]] and [[Millwall F.C.]].
*[[1988]] &ndash; The [[Seikan Tunnel]], the longest undersea tunnel in the world, opens between [[Aomori, Aomori|Aomori]] and [[Hakodate, Hokkaido|Hakodate]], Japan.
*[[1991]] &ndash; The [[United States Department of Justice]] announces that [[Exxon]] has agreed to pay $1&nbsp;billion for the clean-up of the [[Exxon Valdez oil spill]] in Alaska.
*[[1992]] &ndash; The [[Moment magnitude scale|Mw]] 6.7 [[1992 Erzincan earthquake|Erzincan earthquake]] strikes eastern [[Turkey]] with a maximum [[Mercalli intensity scale|Mercalli intensity]] of VIII (''Severe''). At least 498 were killed in this strike-slip event on the [[North Anatolian Fault]].
*[[1996]] &ndash; [[Dunblane school massacre]]: in [[Dunblane]], Scotland, 16 primary school children and one teacher are shot dead by  [[spree killer]] [[Thomas Watt Hamilton]] who then committed suicide.
*[[1997]] &ndash; India's [[Missionaries of Charity]] chooses [[Sister Nirmala]] to succeed [[Mother Teresa]] as its leader.
*  1997   &ndash; The [[Phoenix Lights]] are seen over [[Phoenix, Arizona]] by hundreds of people, and by millions on television.
*[[2003]] &ndash; [[Human evolution]]: The journal ''[[Nature (journal)|Nature]]'' reports that 350,000-year-old footprints of an upright-walking human had been found in Italy.
*[[2008]] &ndash; [[Gold]] prices on the [[New York Mercantile Exchange]] hit $1,000 per ounce for the first time.
*[[2012]] &ndash; At least 28 people are killed in a [[Sierre coach crash|bus crash]] in a motorway tunnel near the town of [[Sierre]] in the Swiss canton of [[Valais]]. 
*[[2013]] &ndash; [[Pope Francis]] is elected, in the [[Papal conclave, 2013|papal conclave]], as the [[List of popes|266th]] [[Pope]] of the [[Catholic Church]].
*[[2014]] &ndash; A [[Haughey Air AgustaWestland AW139 crash|helicopter crash]] in [[Norfolk]], United Kingdom, kills 4 people.
*[[2016]] &ndash; An [[March 2016 Ankara bombing|explosion]] occurs in central [[Ankara]], Turkey, with at least 37 people killed and 127 wounded.
*  2016   &ndash; Three gunmen [[2016 Grand-Bassam shootings|attack]] two hotels in the [[Ivory Coast]] town of [[Grand-Bassam]], killing at least 18 people and injuring 33 others.
